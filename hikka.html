Welcome to Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery - Preserving the Future of Sea Turtles

In the coastal town of Hikkaduwa in Sri Lanka, there is a well-known turtle hatchery and conservation initiative. Through a variety of conservation initiatives, the hatchery seeks to safeguard and maintain sea turtles, a species that is in risk of extinction.

Sea turtle eggs are protected in the hatchery, which offers a secure environment for their development. The staff carefully moves turtle eggs from dangerous nesting locations along the shore to protected regions inside the hatchery. Predation is reduced as a result, and the hatchlings' survival rate is raised.

The Green Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle are among the sea turtle species that may be found in Sri Lanka, and visitors to the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery can learn more about them. Insights into the life cycle of these interesting species and the dangers they encounter in the wild are provided by educational programs and guided tours provided by the hatchery.

The hatchery runs rehabilitation programs for sick or damaged turtles in addition to conservation initiatives. After giving injured turtles medical attention and care, they release them back into the water once they have recovered.

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is essential in promoting awareness of the need to preserve sea turtles' native habitats and the value of sea turtle conservation.Visitors have a rare chance to get up close and personal with these wonderful animals while also helping to preserve them.

Overall, the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a wonderful project that integrates eco-tourism, education, and conservation, making it a must-visit location for Sri Lanka's environment enthusiasts and animal conservationists.
