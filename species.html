TURTLE SPECIES

Welcome to the Enchanting World of Sri Lankan Turtle Species!

Immerse yourself in the captivating realm of Sri Lankan turtle species. Nestled along the pristine shores of this island paradise, our turtle hatcheries provide a sanctuary for these incredible creatures. Discover the intriguing lives of these ancient mariners as they gracefully navigate the turquoise waters of Sri Lanka. From the majestic Leatherbacks to the charming Green Turtles, each species holds a unique tale. Join us on this extraordinary journey to witness their remarkable journey from hatchlings to the vast expanse of the ocean. Prepare to be awed by the beauty, resilience, and sheer wonder of Sri Lanka's magnificent turtles.

Leatherback Turtle

The Leatherback Turtle (Dermochelys coriacea) is the largest turtle species in the world. These magnificent creatures are known for their unique leathery shell, which sets them apart from other turtles. Leatherbacks can be found along the coasts of Sri Lanka, where they come ashore to nest. They are known for their long-distance migrations and their ability to dive to incredible depths. Leatherbacks primarily feed on jellyfish, contributing to the balance of marine ecosystems. Sadly, Leatherback Turtles are critically endangered, and their conservation is of utmost importance.

Green Turtle

The Green Turtle (Chelonia mydas) is a well-known turtle species that frequents the shores of Sri Lanka. These turtles are named after the greenish color of their fat, which is derived from their herbivorous diet. Green Turtles are famous for their beautiful carapace and graceful swimming. They nest on Sri Lanka's sandy beaches, where they dig their nests and lay their eggs. The conservation efforts of hatcheries play a vital role in protecting Green Turtles and ensuring their survival for future generations.

Hawksbill Turtle

The Hawksbill Turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata) is a critically endangered species that can be found in the waters surrounding Sri Lanka. Hawksbills are renowned for their stunningly patterned shells, which are highly valued for their use in jewelry and accessories. These turtles play a crucial role in maintaining the health of coral reefs as they feed on sponges and other invertebrates. Hawksbill Turtles are known to nest on Sri Lanka's beaches, and protecting their nesting sites is essential for their conservation.

Loggerhead Turtle

The Loggerhead Turtle (Caretta caretta) is a globally distributed species that also visits the shores of Sri Lanka. Named for their relatively large heads, Loggerhead Turtles are characterized by their reddish-brown carapace. They are strong swimmers and are known to undertake long migrations. Loggerheads feed on a variety of prey, including crabs, mollusks, and jellyfish. In Sri Lanka, Loggerhead Turtles nest on sandy beaches, and hatcheries play a vital role in protecting their nests and ensuring successful hatching.

Olive Ridley Turtle

The Olive Ridley Turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea) is the most abundant sea turtle species globally and can be found in the waters surrounding Sri Lanka. These turtles are named for their olive-colored carapace. Olive Ridley Turtles display unique mass nesting behavior known as arribadas, where thousands of females come ashore simultaneously to lay their eggs. The conservation efforts of hatcheries are crucial in safeguarding the nests during these nesting events. Olive Ridley Turtles are omnivorous and feed on a variety of marine organisms.
